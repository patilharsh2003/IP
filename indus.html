River Indus

About the River Indus

  • Length: 3,180 kilometers (1,976 miles)
  • Source: The Tibetan Plateau in the Himalayas
  • Mouth: The Arabian Sea
  • Tributaries: The Jhelum, Chenab, Ravi, Sutlej, Beas, and Indus Rivers
  • States it travels through in India: Jammu and Kashmir, Punjab, Haryana, Rajasthan, and Sindh
  • It is the longest river in Pakistan and the second longest river in India.
  • It is one of the most important rivers in the world, providing water for irrigation, drinking water, and transportation.
  • The Indus River Valley Civilization, one of the earliest civilizations in the world, developed along the Indus River.
  • The Indus River is a popular tourist destination, especially for its whitewater rafting and kayaking opportunities.
